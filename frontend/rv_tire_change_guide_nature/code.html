Protocol 04 // Emergency

RV Tire
Recovery.

Field guide for heavy vehicle wheel replacement in off-grid environments. Maintain stability, ensure safety, and return to the trail.

warning

Critical Safety Warning

Never place any part of your body under the RV while it is supported only by a jack. Always use jack stands for secondary support. Ensure all passengers have evacuated the vehicle and are standing at a safe distance.

01

Stabilization

Park on the flattest, firmest ground available. Engage the parking brake and use heavy-duty chocks on the tires opposite the side being changed. If on soft soil, place a wide wooden plank under the jack base to distribute weight.

02

Initial Break

Using your lug wrench, loosen the nuts in a star pattern. Do NOT remove them yet. Only break the initial tension while the tire is still touching the ground to prevent spinning.

build
expand_less
Phase Two

03. Elevation

Position the jack directly under the frame or a designated lift point. Pump slowly and steadily until the flat tire is roughly 3 inches off the ground. Ensure the RV remains vertical and the jack does not lean.

04

Replacement

Finish removing the lug nuts and pull the flat tire toward you. Slide the spare onto the hub, aligning the bolts. Hand-tighten the nuts in the same star pattern used earlier.

05

Grounding

Lower the jack slowly until the tire makes contact. Remove the jack. Use the lug wrench to tighten the nuts as much as possible. Check torque again after driving 50 miles.
